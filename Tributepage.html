Nicolaus Copernicus

Father of modern astronomy

Nicolaus Copernicus was a mathmatician and astronomer who proposed that the sun was stationary in the center of the universe and the earth revolved around it.

Here's a time line of Nicolaus Copernicus:

  • 1473 - Born in Torun, Royal Prussia, Poland
  • 1484 - Copernicus's father dies
  • 1491-1503 - Copernicus studies at various universities in Poland and Italy
  • 1510 - Moves to Frombork, Poland
  • 1514 - Copernicus circulates his Little Commentary, introducing his heliocentric model
  • 1520 - The Teutonic Knights raze Frombork; Copernicus and the other canons work to rebuild the town
  • 1532 - Copernicus completes On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres but waits before publishing it
  • 1543 - Dies in Frombork at age 70, shortly after publication of On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres
